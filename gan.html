Generator vs Discriminator

Two neural networks locked in an adversarial battle — one creates, the other judges. Together, they learn to generate increasingly realistic data.

minG maxD V(D, G) = 𝔼[log D(x)] + 𝔼[log(1 − D(G(z)))]
💡 Pro Tip: This formula is the GAN's "game rule." It says: the Generator (G) tries to minimize while the Discriminator (D) tries to maximize the same score — like a tug-of-war. 𝔼 means "expected value" (average outcome).

The Adversarial Battle

Two competing neural networks, each trying to outsmart the other — this is the core of a GAN.

💡 In Simple Terms: Think of a counterfeiter (Generator) trying to make fake money, and a detective (Discriminator) trying to spot it. As the detective gets better, the counterfeiter must improve too — both push each other to get smarter.

The Adversarial Loss Game

GAN training is a minimax optimization — watch the Generator and Discriminator losses compete in a tug-of-war.

💡 What is "loss"? Loss is a number that measures how wrong a network's predictions are. Lower loss = better performance. In a GAN, when one network's loss goes down, the other's goes up — like a seesaw. The ideal state is Nash Equilibrium — where neither can improve without the other also adapting.
